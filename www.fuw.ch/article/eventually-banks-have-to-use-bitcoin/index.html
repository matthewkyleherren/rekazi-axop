«Eventually, banks have to use Bitcoin»

Wences Casares, founder of Bitcoin startup Xapo, explains, why his company's headqarter is moving from California to Switzerland. He thinks that Bitcoin will be the first native currency of the internet.

Alexander Trentin
Publiziert: 02.07.2015, 16:03
Jetzt abonnieren und von der Vorlesefunktion profitieren.
Abo abschliessenLogin
BotTalk

The 41 year old serial entrepreneur is the CEO of Xapo, a start-up offering Bitcoin vaults. He is orignally from Argentina, but lives and works in Palo Alto, California. Before running Xapo, he founded the first Internet Service Provider (ISP) and the first online broker of Argentina.

Mr. Casares, you are moving your company Xapo to Switzerland. Will you personally also move to Switzerland? - We have moved the company to Zug. I stay in California where we keep a very small office to serve our US customers. All our investors and some of our large customers are based in the US. We are currently looking for a general manager in Zug and we slowly build up a finance team as well as a legal and compliance team.

How would you describe the offering of Xapo right now? - It is very straightforward: We are like a Bitcoin bank. If you have Swiss francs, you can keep them at UBS. But if you have Bitcoins, you can’t keep them in a bank, but you can store them at Xapo. We are the largest custodian of Bitcoins in the world. Our vault product is insured and highly secure. We also offer something similar to a bank account for Bitcoins which makes it very easy for customers to pay with Bitcoins or receive them.

What is the reason to move to Switzerland? - Most of our international customers told us that they don’t want their Bitcoin holdings exposed to the US for various reasons. This is why we have located our main vault for the private keys of Bitcoins in Switzerland. We thought that this would be enough for our customers, but they also asked that the main entity of Xapo would be based outside the US. The alternatives we considered were Singapore, Luxembourg and Switzerland.

Have you contacted the Swiss financial market supervisor FINMA? - Yes, we are working on the regulatory approval right now. We think the Swiss regulatory environment for Bitcoins is very advanced. It might be the best in the world.

Was it important for you that there are already several Bitcoin companies based in Switzerland? - We were surprised how many Bitcoin companies are based in Switzerland. We contacted a number of them. We liked what we heard from them. Their good experience was a reason we decided to move to Switzerland.

How many customers do you currently have? - We are not disclosing the number of customers, only that we are the largest custodian for Bitcoins in the world. We have retail clients as well as institutional funds, including mutual funds, hedge funds and family offices. Even though the institutional clients are less in numbers, they hold a bigger share of the Bitcoin volume.

Does that mean that Bitcoin is an established investment form for these institutional investors? - I don’t think that Bitcoins are a big investment for any of the institutional clients. It is no more than 1% of their portfolios. However, we have a couple of customers who have more than $50 million worth of Bitcoins with us. It is big for us, but not so big for them.

Will you expand your product range? - We have a very ambitious pipe line and we are constantly developing new products. We have already launched a debit card which you can use with merchants or ATMs which accepts credit cards. The money will be automatically deducted from your Xapo account. We are working on a number of products which make it lot easier for people to get access to Bitcoin. We also want to enable people in emerging markets to have access to Bitcoin.

Do you think that banks could use Bitcoin in the future? - Telephone companies didn’t want to touch the internet for many years. I remember talking to Telefónica in 1994, when I was starting the first Internet Service Provider in Argentina. I was telling them that they should pay more attention to the internet. But they thought it would be crazy. The telecommunication companies needed too long to adapt. And that is the situation of the banks today. They should understand that with Bitcoin you can move money all over the world for free. Like telephone companies ignored the internet in the beginning, the banks still ignore Bitcoin. But eventually, they have to use it.

What is your personal motivation to engage in Bitcoin? - I have been working in financial technology my entire career. I have always wondered why technology hasn’t really changed the way finance works, despite all the technological progress. The ATM, credit cards or bank accounts have improved only marginally over the last decades. There are now five billion people who still have to rely on cash as they don’t have access to bank accounts. If we rely on banks, it will stay like that. Bitcoin can change that, especially as many people have mobile phones. To see a world where Bitcoin succeeds, that is what motivates me. Bitcoin is the greatest leap forward in the democratization of money we have ever seen.

Is your engagement also related to your personal experience in Argentina? - When I grew up in Patagonia, my family lost everything multiple times because of inflation. I think Bitcoin is an easy way for people to defend themselves against such abuse.

Is Bitcoin the currency of the future? - Bitcoin will never replace any national currency, but it will become the first global currency. The internet doesn’t have yet its own currency. I imagine a world where Bitcoin is used as the native currency of the internet. And it could replace the dollar as the international standard currency.

Isn’t there the risk that another cryptocurrency could replace Bitcoin? - I don’t think so. Network effects are really important here. A year ago, 50% of all cryptocurrency transactionswere were done with Bitcoin, the other 50% with 90 other currencies. Today, Bitcoin accounts for 96% of all transactions, while only 4% of the transactions are done by 200 different cryptocurrencies. Bitcoin has won: It has 12 million users, and it is adding 50,000 users a day. All other cryptocurrencies together have only 50,000 users. Bitcoin adds as many people in a day as the other currencies have users now.

Xapo’s advisory board is composed of famous people. What do you expect from them? - They all believe in the potential of Bitcoin. We can learn a lot from their know-how and experience. Dee Hock is the founder of Visa, so he knows a lot about creating new payment methods and how to attract merchants and clients. John Reed was responsible for Citibank to become the global retail franchise it is now. He is the guy who made ATMs to be adopted worldwide. And Larry Summers is one of the premier thinkers about the world economy.

Why do customers store Bitcoin with you? - Security is the main concern of our customers. Xapo offers the strongest security technology, and is easy to use. The team has the longest experience in finance in the Bitcoin space. We are not newcomers. We ran banks and financial companies before. We also have the best investors from Silicon Valley, Wall Street and the Bitcoin area.

Are you ahead of your competitors? - We don’t think much about our competition as Bitcoin is not a saturated market. It is much more important to bring new users to Bitcoin instead of attracting users from competitors.
